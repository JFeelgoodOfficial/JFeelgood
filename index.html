The Problem with Generic Art

Most art fills a wall.
Some art fills a void.

"Art that speaks to the deeper aspects of existence will resonate on a profound level."

Your space deserves more than decoration. It deserves conversation — art that makes visitors pause, that invites introspection, that carries weight and meaning long after first glance.

Mass-produced prints and forgettable canvases line the walls of too many homes and offices. They fill space. They never fill meaning.

Original art by JFeelgood does the opposite. Each piece is a meditation: on identity, on resilience, on the human spirit navigating a complex world.

The JFeelgood Difference

Art that is not just
seen, but felt.

JFeelgood's collections span two decades of unflinching self-exploration. From the cosmic solitude of The Lost series to the raw sobriety journals of Self Work, every piece begins with a true story — and ends with yours.

Mixed media on canvas. Gold leaf, epoxy resin, spray paint, acrylic, and found ephemera. Materials that carry memory the way people do.

These are not reproductions. Each work is a one-of-a-kind original. Once it's gone, it's gone — and it carries all the meaning its creation held, ready to carry yours.

Featured in JFeelgood's book Thought Entropy and showcased at venues including Austin Studio Tour, the work has been recognized for its innovation, depth, and emotional honesty.

Meet the Artist

JFeelgood is an accomplished artist with over 18 years of experience across multiple mediums. He has produced several hundred paintings, five published books, five music albums, and a video game.

In 2019, he chose sobriety — and channeled that transformation into his most personal work yet, the Self Work series: an ongoing visual journal of ego, identity, and the continuous process of becoming.

JFeelgood continues to organize community events, lead art organizations, and use creativity as a means of helping others discover their own gifts. His work is also featured in his book Thought Entropy, available on Amazon.
